Classroom-Based Action Research (CBAR)

A reflective process where teachers identify classroom problems and implement targeted interventions.

  • Data-Driven: Decisions based on specific classroom evidence.
  • Iterative: Cycle of Planning, Acting, Observing, and Reflecting.
  • Empowerment: Teachers as active problem-solvers.

While CBAR focuses on issues inside the classroom, we now shift to the outside forces—the community—that influence learner success.

"It takes a village to raise a child."

— African Proverb

"Education is not the filling of a pail, but the lighting of a fire... and that fire needs the oxygen of the whole community to stay alive."

Legal Basis

Education is a Shared Responsibility. The school is not an island; it is the heart of the community ecosystem.

"The State shall ensure that the values, needs and aspirations of a school community are reflected in the program of education for the children. Education shall be the shared responsibility of the entire community."

— Republic Act No. 9155, Section 2

“Aside from teachers and students, who else forms the Wider School Community?”

  • Internal: Administrators, Staff, and Students.
  • External: Parents, Alumni, and Business Leaders.
  • Public Partners: Barangay Officials and Socio-civic Organizations.

The Reality Check

When 90% of parents fail to attend a meeting, we must move from judgment to innovation. Teachers must be Proactive.

“If traditional meetings fail, what Alternative Methods can we use to reach busy parents?”

  • Home Visits: Understanding the learner's environment first-hand.
  • Digital Engagement: Social media groups and SMS for real-time dialogue.
  • Flexible Scheduling: Offering staggered times for working families.

The Next Frontier

Communication helps those already enrolled. But how do we reach the "invisible" learners?

We move from engagement to discovery.

Child Mapping

A data-gathering process to identify potential learners. It ensures the school has an accurate master list of all 4-17 year olds in the vicinity.

The Purpose

The goal is Universal Access. Identifying out-of-school youth allows schools to coordinate with the Barangay Council for intervention.

Required Information

  • Demographics: Name, Age, Birthday, and Address.
  • Educational Status: Grade level or reason for being out of school.
  • Accessibility: Distance to school and mode of transport.
  • Special Needs: Identifying requirements for SPED or inclusive services.
